overview

Design, and code through completion of a responsive website. A minimum of five pages was required. I created a concept design brief that encompassed the needs of a local museum. The pages include the home page, contact page, a visit page, a join page, and an exhibits page. The homepage includes a hero image as well as various dates for events that are coming up. The contact page is very simple and includes a contact form as well as the museum's contact information. The visit page is meant to help visitors plan their trip to the museum. The join page is for getting a membership, donating, or volunteering, and is split up accordingly into those sections. The exhibits page showcases some of the current exhibits.

challenge

Create a completely responsive design utilizing graceful degradation. To layer the features of this website in the form of progressive enhancement. Design a style guide and unify the design across all pages.

approach

I began by researching various museums that curate similar content. I especially looked at how these museums displayed their content in relation to their branding and overall experience. The general overall design emulated the experience of visiting a museum. There was much white space, as well as a neat gridlike layout that was not too rigid. With these principles in mind, I drew up some thumbnails that matched the branding I created for the museum.

conclusion

This concept was tough to design from start to finish. I utilized a number of current web techniques to accomplish the different tasks for this website. There are user interactions that make use of JavaScript to function. The result is a complete responsive website that fulfills all basic requirements of a museum's website.
